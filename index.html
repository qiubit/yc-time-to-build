Chapter 1

Waves have come before

Maybe now is special. Maybe it only feels that way. YC's public batch history gives us a way to look at founder attention without pretending it is destiny.

The pattern is not one continuous trend. Different moments pulled founders toward different bets: web, commerce, fintech, crypto, and now AI / agents.

Chapter 2

The durable waves

Commerce and fintech set the bar. They were not just loud; they became durable company-formation zones over multiple YC batches.

Outcomes take years to mature. That lag matters, because today's formation signal cannot honestly prove tomorrow's winners yet.

Chapter 3

The cautionary wave

Crypto was culturally enormous. Inside YC batch composition, it looks narrower: broad mode never crosses 10% of a batch.

That does not mean crypto failed. It means a loud wave is not automatically a broad founder wave. The chart has to be allowed to complicate the story.

Chapter 4

The current wave

This is the shift.

AI / agents is no longer just a YC category. It is becoming a building block inside other categories.

Broad read: 46.5% of Spring 2026 matched GenAI / agents signals. Strict read: smaller, but still a visible wave.

Not just "AI startups."

B2B, dev tools, fintech, healthcare, and consumer companies are using the same new primitive.

Strict reads tags. Broad also reads descriptions. The real signal lives between them.

Chapter 5

The fork

This is where the data stops pretending.

YC batch composition can show where founders are rushing. It cannot yet show which 2024-2026 companies will endure.

AI / agents might mature like fintech and commerce, stay narrower like crypto, or become so normal that "AI company" stops being the right category.

Chapter 6

The window is open

Formation waves are when founders commit before the market settles. The leading indicator is flashing. The outcome evidence has not had time to exist.

If you are going to build, this is a real moment to build into. The data does not promise the outcome. It does show the rush.

Methodology

What this data can and cannot say

The site uses processed aggregate data from yc-oss/api, downloaded on May 25, 2026. YC tags and one-liners are current public metadata, not frozen batch-time descriptions. Strict mode uses structured taxonomy. Broad mode also reads company descriptions, which can better catch recent agent language but may overcount as profiles drift toward AI.
